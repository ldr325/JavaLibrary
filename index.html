Let me tell you about meat

Meat is great. Well it can be great, if you cook it right and if you source it right. Meat is not just a food group it's an artfrom. As consumers we must be able to ethically source our meat and make sure that there will be enough for others. Meat should be sourced with the animal in mind, taking great care for it and treating it with respect. That being said you must also respect those who are vegan.

I am going to show my three favorite meats throughout this website. They are in no particular order: beef, chicken, goat. You probably expected pork to be on that list but im not a big fan of pork unless it is bacon or pulled pork or in al pastor tacos. I can never seem to digest pork chops or any other grilled pork. I like beef it's not a complicated meat. It can be brought to different flavor levels and its easy to cook. It's quite versatile as well and the cow has many different cuts. Beef stew is also great and you can't not mention filet mignon when talking about beef. In short 8/10

Let's Talk about Chicken

Chicken is also a really versatile meat. It doesnt take a genius to cook it or prepare it. Just be careful to not overcook it or undercook it. Chicken is easy to manipluate, you see it at almost every fast food joint: chicken sandwiches, chicken tenders, chicken nuggets, wings. It's everywhere and goes great with sauces my personal favorite is the polynesian sauce or sweet and sour. Chicken even has it's own fast food chain with Chik-fil-a, however they aren't the best when it comes to social issues. Overall id give chicken a 9/10

Let's Talk about Goats

The only reason i really put goat here is because of the one dish my family makes: barbacoa. It's a really good dish which involves goat being steam cooked in an underground oven. It's unique to mexico and the other latin american countries. It's origin is said to be from the carribbean and diverged from there. This is also where the word barbecue comes from. There are other dishes that can be made from goat but not a lot of people in the US eat them, nor do i beleive people in the US would be willing to introduce goat as a form of meat. Overall 8/10
